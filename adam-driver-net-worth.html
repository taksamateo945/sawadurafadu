Adam Driver Personal And Early Life

Adam Douglas Driver, born on November 19, 1983, hails from San Diego California.

However, he relocated to Mishawaka, Indiana at the age of seven.

Raised by his mother Nancy, a paralegal, and stepfather Rodney G. Wright, a Baptist minister, Driver attended Mishawaka High School in 2001.

He described himself as a misfit, even starting a fight club inspired by the film “Fight Club” with his friends.

In 2001, he graduated and briefly worked as a door-to-door salesman selling Kirby vacuum cleaners.

Following the events of 9/11, Driver joined the United States Marine Corps and served nearly three years.

Unfortunately, he broke his sternum while mountain biking shortly before deployment to Iraq, leading to a medical discharge.

He spent a year at the University of Indianapolis before transferring to Juilliard, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in 2009.

In June 2013, Driver married his longtime girlfriend, Joanne Tucker, whom he met during drama school at Juilliard.

They discreetly welcomed a son two and a half years prior to Driver’s announcement in October 2019.

The family resides in Brooklyn Heights.

Driver also manages a nonprofit organization, Arts in the Armed Forces, which organizes theater performances for military personnel across the U.S. and abroad.

Interestingly, Driver has expressed his refusal to watch or listen to his own performances in several interviews.

During a radio interview with NPR’s “Fresh Air,” he even left the studio mid-interview when the host insisted on playing a clip from “Marriage Story.”

Adam Driver Acting Career

Driver chose to stay in New York City after completing his studies at Juilliard.

He ventured into Broadway and off-Broadway productions while working as a busboy and waiter to make ends meet.

His television debut occurred with a role in “The Unusuals,” and he made his feature film debut in Clint Eastwood’s “J. Edgar.”

Lena Dunham remembered Driver’s distinctive audition and cast him as the emotionally unstable Adam Sackler in her HBO series “Girls,” where he played Hannah Horvath’s boyfriend.

This role marked a turning point in his career, earning him three Emmy nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actor during the show’s run from 2012 to 2017.

Simultaneously, Driver appeared alongside acclaimed actors like Daniel Day-Lewis, Sally Field, David Strathairn, etc in the 2012 film “Lincoln.”

He gained recognition in the off-Broadway scene for his portrayal of Welsh houseguest Cliff in “Look Back in Anger,” earning him the Lucille Lortel Award for Outstanding Featured Actor in a Play.

In 2013, he took on roles in “What If” and “Bluebird.”

His breakthrough in film came with the Coen Brothers’ “Inside Llewyn Davis.”

The subsequent year, Driver appeared in various films, including “Hungry Hearts,” “While We’re Young,” and “This Is Where I Leave You.”

Adam Driver Mainstream Success And Fame

In 2015, Adam Driver gained international fame for his role in “Star Wars: The Force Awakens.” He continued to portray Kylo Ren in “Star Wars:

The Last Jedi” in 2017 and “The Rise of Skywalker” in 2019.

His performance received widespread acclaim, and his character was often considered the standout in the series.

The following year, in 2016, Driver appeared in the sci-fi thriller “Midnight Special” and co-starred in Martin Scorsese’s drama “Silence.”

He wrapped up 2016 with a notable role in Jim Jarmusch’s drama “Paterson,” earning critical acclaim and award nominations.

He won the Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Actor for his performance, and the film featured on numerous critics’ top ten lists of the year’s best films.

In 2017, Driver portrayed a one-armed Iraq War veteran in “Logan Lucky.”

His role in the 2018 movie “BlacKkKlansman” garnered critical praise, earning him an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor and a Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actor.

The same year, Driver starred in Terry Gilliam’s “The Man Who Killed Don Quixote.”

In 2019, he appeared in “The Report” and returned to Broadway to play Pale alongside Keri Russell in “Burn This,” receiving acclaim and a Tony nomination for his powerful performance.

Driver collaborated with Jim Jarmusch once more in the zombie comedy “The Dead Don’t Die,” earning further critical acclaim.

Additionally, in 2019, he co-starred with Scarlett Johansson in Noah Baumbach’s drama “Marriage Story,” receiving overwhelmingly positive acclaim and an Oscar nomination for Best Actor.

Adam Driver’s upcoming projects include starring in “Annette” and two Ridley Scott films.

Beyond film and television acting, he has hosted “Saturday Night Live” three times and provided the voice for Kylo Ren in two video games.
